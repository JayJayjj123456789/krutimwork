3. **Momos (Steamed Dumplings)**  
   Dumplings filled with spiced meat (usually yak, beef, or goat) or vegetables, steamed and served with a chili oil or tomato-based dipping sauce. Influenced by Chinese cuisine but uniquely adapted in Tibet.

4. **Yak Butter Tea (Po Cha)**  
   A salty, creamy beverage made by churning yak butter, tea leaves, and salt. It provides essential calories and warmth in cold climates and is consumed daily across Tibet.

5. **Kapse (Fried Tibetan Pastries)**  
   Deep-fried pastries made from wheat flour, often stuffed with sweet or savory fillings such as black sesame paste or minced meat. They resemble flaky, golden turnovers and are popular snacks.
